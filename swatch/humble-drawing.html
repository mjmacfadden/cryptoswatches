Humble Drawing

by Michael Macfadden

Please introduce yourself to our readers:

HumbleDrawing´s is a team consisting of brother and sister Sára and Kenneth.
Sára is the main artist, and mainly makes traditional physical art pieces, Kenneth is the “tech guy” and sometimes makes digital specials of our original pieces.
We are based in Denmark, but have roots in the Faroe Islands.
Sára is working full time as a nurse, and has been drawing and painting on a hobby basis for most of her life. Kenneth works full time as a fabricator/welder

What was the inspiration for this piece?

A lot of the inspiration either comes spontaneously or from the beauty in the nature all around us, this shows up on quite a few of our released pieces, and is the case for this humble Buttercup flower as well.
Even if it might be considered just to be weeds by some, it still holds a ton of beauty

How long have you been creating in the digital realm?

We have been NFT´s for right about six months at this point, for Sára it´s over 25 years of drawing and painting on a hobby basis

Can you tell us about your workflow (what equipment and software do you use to create)?

For our physical pieces it varies which paints and canvases are used, a lot of pieces are made on canvas with acrylic and other types of paints, also a lot of pieces are made on paper also with different paints, watercolors etc.
Most of the materials are from the local hobbyist store, not always the top of the line stuff, but there isn´t really a need for using expensive things to obtain amazing results.
For the digital pieces we make, we have been using GIMP and After Effects, Kenneth has recently embarked upon the journey of learning Blender as well, as we´d love to make some more animated pieces in the future.

Who are your artistic influences?

It´s very tough to pinpoint any single artist, there are simply just so many talented artists and creators in the space, it´s almost daily we stumble upon someone who makes something amazing, different or somehow not seen before.
The easy answer would be to say nature, as it really does have a big influence on some of our pieces

What got you interested in NFTs/Crypto Art/Digital Collectibles?

Kenneth has been in crypto on and off since 2010, and the interest for NFT´s spiked back in late January this year as it was mentioned by a friend what NFT´s were, Kenneth started looking into WAX, and began collecting different NFT´s.
