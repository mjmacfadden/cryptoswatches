FeltnerIII

by Michael Macfadden

Please introduce yourself to our readers. 

My name is David Feltner III, professionally I have been in the finance field for the past 20 years. While not working I have been creating art over the same period of time. I have kept all of my art to myself until recently. NFTs gave me the media and inspiration to get into NFTs.

What was the inspiration for this piece? 

I was inspired to create this piece because I love skulls and wanted to make something different from my normal project, which is more cartoonish and fun.

How long have you been creating in the digital realm? 

Digitally I have been creating for about 2 months.

Can you tell us about your workflow? 

My typical workflow consists of small amounts of brainstorming prior to putting my focus on to paper, canvas or digitally. Depending on my mood I will mix medias and combine them to create a truly unique digital piece. When I digitally edit my works I use Adobe, 3D Paint, Pixma & Open Shot amongst others. I am always trying new programs and experimenting with the limits of each.

Who are your artistic influences? 

I don’t have much of an art background to be honest. I am familiar with major works of art from Leonardo and other old world Greek and Roman artists. I draw from those styles, as they have fantasy type feel. I really love the imagery in Dungeons & Dragons.

What got you interested in NFTs/Crypto Art/Digital Collectibles?

I first got introduced to crypto about 6 months ago. I started watching BTC and ETH. I jumped on some Doge and Stellar Lumens, which I still Hodl. I saw that NFTs were expanding and I thought it would be a great way to express myself and my art. I love the collectibles that are out, so I am working on a series of 100 1/1 characters. The series is called the 100 Goobers. I will be dropping my 20th Goober 07/31/2021 and I am very proud of that.
