CornchipsDCS

by Michael Macfadden

Please introduce yourself to our readers. What would you like them to know about you?

My name is Matt Hill and I’m a signwriter, designer, artist, animation rookie, website building and marketing enthusiast… Basically a creative that keeps learning new applications for his ideas. From Sydney Australia.

What was the inspiration for this piece?

The Damn Tasty Corn Chips was an art project that was meant for the walls of Mexican restaurants and Inner Sydney suburb cafes. I was planning on printing and framing them and trying to sell the art on consignment. Then I fell into NFTs.

How long have you been creating in the digital realm?

Since I started signwriting I was designing digital artworks professionally from 1998. But was was playing with art programs and underdeveloped Photoshop type apps since we had our Compaq Presario when I was maybe 10. Which was early 1990s.

Can you tell us about your workflow?

I use Adobe illustrator, Photoshop, premiere pro… There’s no work flow really. Just chaotic realisations of ideas I want to see outside my brain. The tools help to create those images.

Who are your artistic influences?

Mr Squiggle The man from the moon, Blackbeard ltd, Jack Daniels and a lot of time staring into nothing thinking what I should do next.

What got you interested in NFTs/Crypto Art/Digital Collectibles?

A friend told me I should look into it when I was creating racing liveries. Another friend @dedhedartist showed me how.

Where can we find you online?

Dayum.com.au, Cornchips.io and all socials are @cornchipsdcs

Anything else you’d like to share?

I’ll keep doing what I do even if no one buys it. I love what I do.

Thanks so much for the Collab! The people in the NFT space, wax especially is a brilliant community and most want to work together.
