SADU ONE

by Michael Macfadden

Please introduce yourself to our readers:

My name is Paul but the Metaverse knows me as PV or PVM. I started as a crypto content creator on Publish0x, and
expanded on many other platforms. I share my time between Romania and the UK, as any place with internet is a good
place to live.

I entered the cryptoworld in 2015, when I bought and sold some Bitcoin. Took a break between 2017 and 2020, when I
made my comeback! Since March 2020 I published an article every day, heading towards a 1000 consecutive days
streak.

What was the inspiration for this piece?

Harvest Finance was the first DeFi project I tried, and the “humble farmers” movement made me explore decentralized
finance even more. The tractor I used for Humble Tractors (https://opensea.io/collection/humble-tractors) exists IRL,
and was my source of inspiration for many creativity contests. I wanted to make it live forever on the blockchain and
minted it as my first collection on Opensea. Funny enough, my first NFT on WAX was also Harvest Finance related.

How long have you been creating in the digital realm?

I created some NFTs on WAX just for fun since 2021, as a way to try my skills or to mark various events. I created the
blockstars21 collection (https://wax.atomichub.io/explorer/collection/blockstars21) and minted NFTs and shared them as
rewards for competitions or giveaways, a concept that is similar to what POAP is now.

Can you tell us about your workflow?

I don’t use any software for my creations, using only paper, pencils and colours. I feel that this is the original indie way,
something that makes my work both unique and special.

Who are your artistic influences?

Definitely Byron! I know my way around with a pencil but watching his videos and understanding his style made me
learn some tricks of the trade.

What got you interested in NFTs/Crypto Art/Digital Collectibles?

I was “corrupted” by NFTs instantly, as the young PVM had an impressive collection of Panini albums, stickers and
other collectibles. I had so many stickers from gums, including Garbage Pale Kids and Turbo cars, so the NFT concept
was mind blowing. It all started with one CryptomonKey and one Byron NFT, back in 2021, and it went all the way to
over 5000 NFTs on WAX, Ethereum, Polygon, Solana and SmartBCH.
