CorpWorld_NFT

by Michael Macfadden

Please introduce yourself to our readers:

Hello, I am Jonezy, creator of Corporate World (Hell on Earth). I am a self-taught artist. When I am not on the laptop, you would see me sketching, building LEGOs, or playing console games. I love the process of creation and building something.

What was the inspiration for this piece?

BeelzeBoss is the main character of the Corporate World collection. He came about when I did a doodle of a demon in a business suit holding a contract. Found the doodle again and poof it clicked!

He’s the CEO of the H.E.L.L. company. His unique aura leads to a literally toxic environment. It morphed the employees into their worst versions of themselves.

The whole project is somewhat a satirical look at workplace life, but I have exaggerated it a tad bit.

How long have you been creating in the digital realm?

I have been creating in the digital realm for a long time. I do illustrations, graphic design and created websites and apps for different companies.

Can you tell us about your workflow (what equipment and software do you use to create)?

I usually start with pencil/paper and sketch out a rough of the character. Then, I scan/take a picture of it then import it to Procreate on the iPad. Once in Procreate, I clean up the sketch and make a better sketch, then move on to inking and coloring. Once done, I move it to Photoshop where I lay it out on the card/NFT.

Who are your artistic influences?

There are so many artists to mention. Anything weird or visually stunning appeals to me. Amongst the various influences are pop surrealism/lowbrow art, vintage comics, retro animations, Japanese manga/anime, graffiti, and more.

What got you interested in NFTs/Crypto Art/Digital Collectibles?

I have always liked the idea of creating my own collectibles. Ever since I am a kid. I tend to come up with weird characters and create comics or collector cards made out of paper/cardboard. Now that NFTs are here, I have a digital medium to spread my ideas and characters.
